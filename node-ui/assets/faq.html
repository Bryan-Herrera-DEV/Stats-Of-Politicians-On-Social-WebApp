Sure. It is the first version of the real project. It will be extended with other social media and countries' statistics. Over time, further statistics will be introduced using Machine Learning.

The project aims to equally compare all the political groups officially present in the Parliament. For equality, all political groups must have the same number of components. For each political group, I have chosen the top 7 accounts using two criteria: number of followers and verified profile.

For instance, let's consider two groups: A and B with 10 and 350 associated politicians, respectively - of whom 5 and 17 are on social and use them frequently - and suppose to analyse all of them. Suppose A and B have a mediocre and an excellent trend, respectively. If we analyse all accounts, probably their statistics will be similar and staggered since statistics are weighted on the number of analysed accounts.

I analyse just Twitter because it easily allows data fetching (under specific conditions). Other social like Facebook and Instagram have more rigid rules to do. Anyway, don't worry, in time I will try to provide you ever more social and statistics.

The "sentiment" field has been estimated using VADER. VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner) is a lexicon and rule-based sentiment analysis tool that is specifically attuned to sentiments expressed in social media.
Yes, it is accurate. VADER outperforms individual human raters with an F1 Classification Accuracy = 0.96 and 0.84, respectively.

Hutto, C.J. & Gilbert, E.E. (2014). VADER: A Parsimonious Rule-based Model for Sentiment Analysis of Social Media Text. Eighth International Conference on Weblogs and Social Media (ICWSM-14). Ann Arbor, MI, June 2014.
